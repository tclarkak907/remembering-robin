Her life and her love

Robin’s Story

Robin Gail Clark was born in Anchorage, Alaska, on February 9, 1961. She filled her 64 years with family, generosity, laughter, hard-earned wisdom, and a determination to find the fun whenever it was hiding.

Children naturally found their way to Robin. Her sons’ friends, her nephews, and many others knew her as a second mom. She cared instinctively, celebrated every little victory as though it deserved a parade, and wanted good things for the people she loved.

Her three sons, Tim, Shane, and Jeremy, were her world. That love grew again through her eight grandchildren: Caylee, Sierra, Aiden, Josephine, Mya, Isabelle, Jace, and Melia.

Robin’s working life was woven through family. She was a florist, helped her father Dick and brother Rick manage the family detail business, and later made candles at her sister Linda’s shop. The work mattered, but being together mattered more.

Alaska always held her heart. She especially loved Monti Bay in Yakutat, where her grandparents Bill and Alice Hansen made memories with her. The ocean called to her, and orca whales held a special place in her imagination.

Robin is remembered by her sons and grandchildren; her siblings Dawn Lawton, Linda Waite, and Rick McCracken; her nieces and nephews; her mother, Dee Walsh; and the many people who still hear her laughter in their favorite stories.

Her life held both love and struggle, but her care for family endured through it all. She is deeply missed, forever loved, and absolutely not interested in us spending the whole day crying.
